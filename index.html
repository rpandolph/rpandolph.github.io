I'm Rebecca, a data analyst with managerial experience

As a data analyst with more than 10 years of work experience, I am passionate about making data accessible and empowering its users and consumers. I’ve worked in a variety of industries covering manufacturing, agriculture, and non-profits. As a result, I’ve learned how to quickly learn about a new industry to provide meaningful insights to my employers and clients, using a variety of skills. These skills include simple and effective data visualizations and collecting data from primary and secondary data sources. I use programming languages like SQL, Python, R and MS Office to automate and replicate data collection, analysis, and dissemination.

While my skill set is primarily in data analysis, my more recent role has been at a managerial level. The experience I've gained includes, hiring and onboarding new staff, performance reviews, managing a budget for my section, and data management. I've also provided training both in-house and with external parties both as a subject matter expert but also in relation to career development.

Presentations & Writings

The following are links of writing samples with data visualizations as well as presentations where I was a guest speaker.

Coffee Market Report-December 2020

This report covers the coffee market (prices, supply, demand) with a focus on coffee trade for major consumer countries. All charts were made in Microsoft Excel, including its multi-level category chart to show imports and re-exports by type of coffee and country.

Coffee Market Report-November 2019

This is another monthly coffee market report but focuses on exports by type of coffee from coffee producing regions. Trade flows are shown in a sankey chart made in ggplot2

Coffee Development Report-Part III

I was responsibile for editing and writing part III of this publication, which provided an overview of the coffee market in 2019/20. This included the outbreak of covid-19 mid-way through the 2019/20 marketing year. The section on coffee market price volatility was done in collaboration with the International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI)
